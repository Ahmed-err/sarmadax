## 2. Positioning

### Market Position
Premium quality at a lean price. Sarmadax sits above the freelancer market (higher craft, full product thinking) and below large agencies (faster, leaner, no overhead). The target zone is clients who have outgrown Fiverr but don't need a 10-person agency.

### Core Differentiator
Design **and** engineering in one place. Most developers hand off design work. Most designers hand off development. Sarmadax does both at a high level internally — which means tighter feedback loops, more coherent products, and no "lost in translation" between design and code.

### Target Clients
| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| **Startup founders** | Pre-seed to Series A, need an MVP or first product fast |
| **SMB owners** | Established business wanting a modern web presence or internal tool |
| **Product managers** | Inside larger companies needing an external build partner |
| **International clients** | Arabic-speaking market (MENA) + global English-speaking market |

### What we do
- SaaS product development (MVP → scale)
- Custom web applications
- Mobile apps (React Native, iOS & Android)
- UI/UX design & branding
- AI integration & automation
- API development & integrations
- E-commerce solutions
- Maintenance & ongoing support

### What we don't do
- Cheap, rushed work
- Projects without a discovery call
- Scope that keeps growing without agreement
- Work where the client disappears mid-project

To help founders and businesses turn their ideas into scalable digital products — built with care, shipped fast, owned by them.

No shortcuts. Every line of code, every pixel, every interaction is deliberate. We'd rather take one more day than ship something we're not proud of.

You own the code, the IP, the repository. We build it, you keep it. Zero lock-in.

**Speed without sacrifice**  
Weeks, not months. We run lean processes, avoid unnecessary meetings, and ship. Fast doesn't mean careless — it means focused.

**Partnership over vendor**  
We're invested in outcomes, not just deliverables. If something in the plan isn't serving your goals, we'll tell you.

## 4. Brand Personality

### Archetype
**The Craftsperson** — precise, capable, quietly confident. Does the work, shows the result, lets quality speak.

### Personality Traits
- **Confident, not arrogant** — We know what we're good at. We don't oversell it.
- **Direct, not blunt** — Clear communication. No fluff, no jargon for its own sake.
- **Premium, not pretentious** — The work is world-class. The attitude is approachable.
- **Technical, not intimidating** — We understand complexity but translate it for non-technical clients.
- **Warm, not casual** — Professional but human. We care about the people we work with.

### What Sarmadax sounds like
- Honest and plain-spoken
- Focused on outcomes, not features
- Confident without chest-beating
- Uses "we" but acknowledges the boutique/solo reality naturally

### What Sarmadax does NOT sound like
- Buzzword-heavy agency speak ("synergize", "leverage", "holistic solutions")
- Overly casual or meme-heavy
- Corporate and distant
- Pushy sales language

## 6. Visual Identity

### Logo
The Sarmadax mark is a stylized code/bracket icon paired with the wordmark in **Geist Sans Bold**. The icon sits in a gradient container (blue → teal). The wordmark is always in the same weight and tracking — never stretched, never outlined.

**Logo do's:**
- Use on dark backgrounds (primary context)
- Use on solid light backgrounds
- Use on the brand gradient (white version)
- Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the "C" in Sarmadax on all sides

**Logo don'ts:**
- Don't change the colors of the icon
- Don't use on busy photographic backgrounds without a backing
- Don't recreate in different fonts
- Don't apply drop shadows or effects

### Design Aesthetic

**Dark-first glass morphism.** The primary visual language is:
- Deep dark backgrounds with layered transparency
- `backdrop-filter: blur(16–24px)` glass cards
- Subtle gradient borders and glow effects
- Blue → teal gradient as the primary energy/action color
- Smooth, physics-based animations (spring + ease curves)

**Motion principles:**
- Ease: `cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)`
- Duration: 300–600ms for reveals, 150–250ms for interactions
- Stagger: 60ms per item in lists/grids
- Entry: fade + translateY(24px) → 0
- CTAs: shimmer sweep animation on hover

**Spatial system:**
- Base unit: 4px
- Component padding: 20–32px
- Section spacing: 80–100px
- Max content width: 1280px (`max-w-7xl`)

## 8. Pricing Philosophy

- **Fixed-price packages** — no ambiguity, no hourly surprises
- **50/50 payment split** — 50% to start, 50% on final approval (client holds leverage)
- **Milestone-based** — progress tied to deliverables, not calendar time
- **No hidden fees** — scope changes require explicit agreement before billing

### Packages (as of launch)
| Package | Price | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Spark** | $1,500 | Landing page or marketing site (5–7 days) |
| **Launch** | $4,500 | Full web app or SaaS MVP (3–4 weeks) |

## 9. Brand Don'ts (Summary)

- Don't claim to be an "agency" with a team if the conversation is personal — lean into boutique/specialist positioning
- Don't use dark patterns or urgency tricks (fake "only 2 spots left" if not true)
- Don't take projects where the client is disrespectful or unresponsive during discovery
- Don't compromise quality to hit a budget — offer a smaller scope instead
- Don't rebrand or change visual identity per client request — the brand stays consistent across all touchpoints

## 10. Brand Checklist (for new touchpoints)

Before publishing any new piece of brand material, verify:

- [ ] Headline leads with outcome, not feature
- [ ] Fixed-price / no-surprise messaging present where relevant
- [ ] Uses gradient accent correctly (not overused)
- [ ] Dark-first design, glass aesthetic consistent with site
- [ ] Geist Sans for Latin, Cairo for Arabic
- [ ] No buzzwords or agency filler copy
- [ ] Ownership / IP transfer point surfaced (key trust signal)
- [ ] CTA is specific and low-friction (free call, not "contact us")
